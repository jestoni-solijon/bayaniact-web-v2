Data Privacy

1. Introduction

This Privacy Policy outlines how BayaniAct collects, uses, discloses, and protects your personal information when you interact with our services. We are committed to protecting your privacy and ensuring your personal information is handled in accordance with applicable privacy laws, including the Data Privacy Act of 2012 (Republic Act No. 10173) of the Philippines.

By accessing or using our system, you agree to the collection and use of your personal information as described in this policy. If you do not agree with this policy, please do not use our services.

2. Information We Collect

We collect personal information that is necessary for the provision of our services. The types of personal information we collect include:

  • Personal Identification Information:
    • Name (Full name)
    • Address (Barangay, City, Province)
    • Contact Information (Phone number, Email address)
    • Date of Birth
    • Gender
    • Civil Status (if required for specific services)
    • Profile Picture (optional, if applicable)
  • Service-Related Information:
    • Request Type (e.g., Barangay Clearance, Certificate of Residency, etc.)
    • Purpose of Request (e.g., Employment, Scholarship, Legal Requirement)
    • Business Information (if requesting a Business Permit Clearance)
    • Payment Information (if applicable for paid services)
  • Device and Usage Information:
    • IP Address
    • Browser type and version
    • Operating system
    • Cookies and other tracking technologies (for analytics and functionality purposes)
  • Other Information:
    • Feedback or complaints (submitted through the platform)
    • Communication with Barangay officials (if applicable)
3. How We Use Your Information

We use the information we collect for the following purposes:

  • To provide services: Process and fulfill requests for certificates, clearances, and other barangay services.
  • To communicate with you: Respond to inquiries, complaints, and feedback, and send notifications about your service requests.
  • For payment processing: If applicable, process payments for services such as certificates or clearances.
  • To improve our services: Analyze usage patterns to enhance and customize the functionality and features of the Barangay Management System.
  • For legal and regulatory purposes: Comply with legal obligations, including reporting and record-keeping requirements.
4. How We Protect Your Information

We take the security of your personal information seriously. We implement a range of administrative, physical, and technical measures to protect your personal data from unauthorized access, alteration, disclosure, or destruction. These measures include:

  • Encryption: We use encryption protocols to protect sensitive information during transmission.
  • Access Control: Only authorized personnel have access to your personal information.
  • Secure Servers: We store your data on secure servers with industry-standard protections.
  • Regular Audits: We conduct regular security audits to ensure the integrity of our data protection practices.
5. Sharing Your Information

We do not sell, rent, or trade your personal information to third parties. However, we may share your personal data in the following circumstances:

  • Barangay Officials and Administrators: Your information may be shared with authorized barangay officials for the purpose of processing and fulfilling your service requests.
  • Service Providers: We may share your data with third-party service providers (e.g., payment processors) who assist in providing services related to the BMS. These providers will only have access to the data necessary to perform their functions and are required to protect your information.
  • Legal Compliance: We may disclose your personal information when required by law, such as to comply with a subpoena, legal process, or regulatory requirement.
6. Cookies and Tracking Technologies

We use cookies and other tracking technologies to enhance your experience when using our system. Cookies are small text files that are stored on your device, which help us analyze usage patterns and personalize content.

You can configure your browser settings to reject cookies or alert you when cookies are being sent. However, if you disable cookies, certain features of the system may not work properly.

7. Your Rights and Choices

Under the Data Privacy Act of 2012, you have the following rights regarding your personal information:

  • Right to Access: You have the right to request access to the personal information we hold about you.
  • Right to Rectification: You have the right to correct any inaccuracies in your personal information.
  • Right to Erasure: You have the right to request the deletion or destruction of your personal data, subject to legal obligations.
  • Right to Object: You may object to the processing of your personal data in certain circumstances.
  • Right to Data Portability: You may request a copy of your personal data in a structured, commonly used, and machine-readable format.

To exercise these rights, please contact us using the contact information provided below. We will respond to your request within a reasonable timeframe.

8. Retention of Data

We retain your personal information only for as long as is necessary to fulfill the purposes outlined in this Privacy Policy or as required by law. After this period, we will securely delete or anonymize your data.

9. Third-Party Links

Our system may contain links to third-party websites or services that are not operated by us. We are not responsible for the privacy practices or content of these third-party sites. We encourage you to read the privacy policies of any third-party websites you visit.
